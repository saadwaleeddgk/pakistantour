Shandoor Polo Festival

The Shandoor Polo Festival is an annual event held at the Shandoor Pass, a high mountain plateau located in Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan. It is one of the highest polo grounds in the world, situated at an altitude of over 3,700 meters (12,000 feet) above sea level. The festival is a celebration of the ancient sport of polo, often referred to as the "sport of kings," which has been played in the region for centuries. Teams from different regions of Gilgit-Baltistan and neighboring Chitral compete in exhilarating polo matches, showcasing their skill, agility, and horsemanship. In addition to polo matches, the Shandoor Polo Festival features cultural performances, traditional music, dance, and handicrafts, providing visitors with a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the region. The festival also offers opportunities for adventure enthusiasts to trek, camp, and explore the breathtaking landscapes surrounding the Shandoor Pass. The Shandoor Polo Festival is not just a sporting event; it is a celebration of tradition, culture, and the indomitable spirit of the people of Gilgit-Baltistan. It brings communities together, fosters goodwill and camaraderie, and promotes tourism and economic development in the region.
