Peshawari Chapal

Peshawari Chapal is a traditional footwear style originating from Peshawar, the capital city of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK), Pakistan. Known for its simplicity, durability, and comfort, Peshawari Chapal is a popular choice of footwear for both men and women in the region. Peshawari Chapal is characterized by its distinctive design, featuring a flat sole made from leather or rubber, and two broad straps that crisscross over the foot and fasten with buckles or buttons. The straps are often adorned with intricate stitching or embroidery, adding a touch of elegance to the sandals. Peshawari Chapal is not just footwear but a symbol of cultural identity and pride, worn by people of all ages and backgrounds in KPK and beyond. It is versatile enough to be paired with traditional attire for formal occasions or worn casually with everyday outfits. The craftsmanship and quality of Peshawari Chapal have earned it a reputation for excellence, with artisans using traditional techniques passed down through generations to create each pair by hand. Whether strolling through bustling bazaars or attending festive gatherings, Peshawari Chapal remains a timeless and beloved accessory for those who appreciate comfort, style, and tradition.
